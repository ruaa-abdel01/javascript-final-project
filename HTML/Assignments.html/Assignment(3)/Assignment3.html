The Power Of Semantic HTML

A demonstration of heading hierarchy and paragraph styling for clean, accessible, and visually appealing web content.

Why Headings Matter

Headings (h1-h6) provide structure to your content, much like chapters and sections in a book. They help both users and search engines understand the flow of information.

The Hierarchy Principle

Always maintain a logical order. Never skip heading levels as it confuses the readers.

Visual vs. Semantic Importance

Use CSS to control the appearance, but preserve semantic meaning in your HTML structure.

Pro Tip: Less Is More

Limit use of h5/h6 to niche cases like disclaimers or footnotes.

Accessibility Note

Screen readers rely on headings for navigation. Test with tools like WAVE or AXE.

Paragraph Styling

Well-spaced paragraphs with a comfortable line-height (1.5-1.6) improve readability. Use CSS to enhance the visual appeal without compromising clarity.
